# Pricing strategy — OS Kitchen

Maps to in-app plan enums (`STARTER`, `PRO`, `TEAM`, `ENTERPRISE`). Adjust Stripe Price IDs separately.

## Positioning

Anchor on **labor saved at cutoff** and **fewer missed channel orders**, not “another dashboard.”

## Suggested public tiers

| Plan | Who | Anchor price idea | Includes |
|------|-----|-------------------|----------|
| **Starter** | Solo chef testing preorder | Low entry | Manual orders, 1 active menu, tight monthly order cap, basic production |
| **Pro** | Growing meal prep / bakery preorder | Core revenue | Woo + Shopify connectors, analytics, inventory lite, higher order cap |
| **Team** | Ghost kitchen / multi-station | Premium | Uber Eats + Uber Direct modules, roles, webhook tooling, unlimited orders |
| **Enterprise** | Multi-location, SLA | Custom | Dedicated support, custom integrations, API/SLA |

## Packaging notes

- Lead with **annual** pricing; monthly as anchor +20%.
- Add **concierge onboarding** as paid add-on for first 50 customers.
- Usage warnings before hard blocks (upgrade modal).

## Competitive framing

- Cheaper than hiring one shift lead hour/day on spreadsheet cleanup.
- Comparable category: ops add-ons to Shopify, not full ERP.

## Experiments

- 14-day trial with demo import CTA on day 1.
- Credit card optional until channel connect (reduce friction).
