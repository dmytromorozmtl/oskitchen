## Minute 0–2: Intro

- "Thanks for taking the time!"
- "Tell me about your kitchen — what do you do, how many orders per week, what's your biggest headache right now?"
- **Listen for:** preorder vs walk-in, number of brands, delivery apps, spreadsheet pain

**Qualify out early if:** needs offline card terminal, native Uber/DoorDash sync day-one, SSO/SOC2

---

## Minute 2–5: Show the problem

- "Most kitchens I talk to struggle with [their specific pain — orders from multiple channels, production planning, packing mistakes]."
- "Let me show you how OS Kitchen handles this."

**Mirror their words** — use their channel names (Shopify, walk-in, corporate lunch, etc.)

---

## Objection Handling

### "We already use Toast/Square"

> Toast is great for front-of-house. OS Kitchen is built for the **kitchen** — production, packing, inventory. Most of our pilots use both: Toast for payments, OS Kitchen for everything after the order comes in.

See also: [GTM_SALES_PLAYBOOK.md](../GTM_SALES_PLAYBOOK.md)

### "It's too expensive"

> Our Starter plan is **$29/month**. Most kitchens save that in the first week from fewer packing mistakes and clearer production planning.

### "I need to think about it"

> Totally fair. I'll send you a link to explore on your own: **https://os-kitchen.com/demo**. Can I check in next week?

### "Do you integrate with Uber Eats / DoorDash?"

> Today we support WooCommerce and Shopify import, plus manual order entry. Native delivery marketplace sync is on the roadmap — we'll be transparent about what's live vs beta during pilot.

### "Do you work offline?"

> Cash POS can queue offline and sync when back online. Card payments need connectivity — we're web-first BYOD, not proprietary terminals.
